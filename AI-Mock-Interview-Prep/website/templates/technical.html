Acing technical interviews requires a mix of preparation, problem-solving skills, and communication. Here are key strategies to succeed:

Master the Fundamentals: Ensure a solid grasp of data structures (arrays, linked lists, stacks, queues, hash tables) and algorithms (sorting, searching, dynamic programming, recursion). Know time and space complexities (Big O Notation) for evaluating performance.

Practice Problem Solving: Regularly solve coding challenges on platforms like LeetCode, HackerRank, or Codeforces. Focus on common problems related to arrays, strings, graphs, and trees.

Study Company-Specific Patterns: Research the kinds of questions commonly asked at your target companies. Practice mock interviews that simulate their style and difficulty level.

Focus on Clarity and Communication: During the interview, talk through your thought process. Explain how you're breaking down the problem, why you're choosing a certain approach, and any trade-offs you're considering.

Optimize Your Solutions: Once you've provided an initial solution, discuss ways to optimize it. Interviewers often look for your ability to improve code performance.

Stay Calm and Think Aloud: If you're stuck, stay composed, think out loud, and ask clarifying questions. This shows your problem-solving approach and can provide hints from the interviewer.

Work on System Design (for senior roles): For more experienced roles, prepare for system design interviews by studying scalable system architectures, design patterns, databases, and cloud solutions.

Practice your coding!
